My first job was at a .NET ship. I knew a little bit of Visual Basic and I had heard of ASP.Net. No joke. When I was being interviewed at my first job I was asked "Is your .NET experience in WebForms or MVC?" I stared blankly and answered "Web...Forms...?" The interviewer looked back at me and said "That's exactly what we use here. When can you start?"

I left and bought a book called "30 Days to learn WebForms". I went through that entire book in 5 days.

When I started that Monday I felt really good about myself. I walked into the office at 8:30am. A man asked me who I was. I told them I was a new hire. He looked skeptical and asked me if I was in the right place. I told him George had hired me last week. He said George wasn't here today and again asked me if I was sure I was in the right place. I told him I was. He looked at me for a few seconds and finally said "Okay. Good enough for me. Let's get you a computer."

I was walked back into the a big cubical room and given my own little slice at a large connected desk. Another man walked me through setting up my computer. He checked the code for my project using Team Foundation Server. He opened up a file, it had over 50,000 lines of code in it. My heart sank. The man said "This should all look pretty standard to you."

"Oh my god. My college professors were right. The real world is really freaking hard."

My mind began to race. My heart was beating rapidly. "Oh my god. My college professors were right." I thought. "The real world is really freaking hard. There's probably every sorting algorthm implemented from scratch in here. I should have studied harder!"

Little did I know that the file was generated by an ORM. No one could read that code.
