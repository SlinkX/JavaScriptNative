Harriet Hosmer

Lady Sculptor

Harriet Hosmer was born on October 9, 1830 in Watertown, Massachusetts. From a young age she showed an affinity for sculpting. Her father recognized her skill and encouraged her studies. She became the most distinguished female sculptor of her time.

  • 1852 – Harriet, her father, and friend Charlotte Cushman head to Italy. Harriet continued her sculpture studies.
  • 1856 – She delivered her first commissioned work.
  • 1860 – Commissioned by the state of Missouri to produce a bronze statue of Senator Thomas Hart Benton.
  • 1861 – Harriet would produce many sculptures for next few decades.
  • 1894 – Her final major work was unveiled. A statue of the Queen of Spain commissioned by the city of San Francisco.
  • 1908 – Harriet died at age 77 in Watertown, Massachusettes.
