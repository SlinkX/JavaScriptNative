Fundamentals of Physics

with Albert Einstein

Physics governs our everyday lives and is involved in a number of activities we perform and things we use in our daily life. Physics is often considered as an immersive yet challenging subject. It is one of the most fundamental scientific disciplines. The main goal of physics is to explain how things move in space and time. This course provides a thorough introduction to the principles and methods of physics for students who have good preparation in physics and mathematics. We will explore the fundamental aspects of physics in a very interactive format. We will understand the nature and scope of the subject, differenmt types of Force and Motion, Energy and Power, Gravitation, Heat, Waves, Sound waves, Light, the Theory of Relativity and many more.

Introduction to Classical Literature

with Charles Dickens

From Homer to Troy, explore classic literature and culture depicting the classical age. This course examines literary works within their historic and social context and within the context of universal values and concerns of the human experience. The focus is on those authors of great works who have been a continuing influence on literature and culture. It offers students a wide-ranging grounding in classical literature as a basis for the further study of Western literature within a comparative framework. Major works of ancient Greek and Roman literature are studied in order to enable students to appreciate literary engagement with the classical world as well as some of the major genres of Western literature: tragedy, comedy and the epic.

Introduction to Political Science

with Martin Luther King

Do you want to know more about political behaviour, understand the practice of public relations or begin your career in politics? Before you know it, you could be well on the way to becoming a successful political scientist. Political science focuses on the theory and practices of governing bodies as well as the analysis of political processes. As an academic discipline, it encompasses the study of different systems of government, election processes, political parties, political ideology, historical analysis, changes in power, public policy, international relations, key social science concepts, and much more. With the breadth of focus areas in political science, aspiring professionals can hone in on a job role that best aligns with their interests.
